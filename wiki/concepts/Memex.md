# Memex

**One-line definition:** Vannevar Bush's 1945 vision of a personal, curated knowledge store with associative trails between documents — a direct ancestor of the LLM Wiki pattern.

## Explanation

Described in Bush's 1945 essay "As We May Think," the Memex was a hypothetical desk-sized device where an individual could store all their books, records, and communications, with the ability to create "associative trails" linking related items. The idea was that the web of connections between documents was as valuable as the documents themselves.

Bush's Memex was personal and actively curated — closer to the LLM Wiki pattern than to what the web became (public, crawled, link-based but not personally maintained).

The problem Bush couldn't solve: **who does the maintenance?** Building and maintaining associative trails is tedious work. The LLM Wiki pattern answers this: the LLM does the maintenance.

## Tensions & Contradictions

The web partially realized Bush's vision at a global scale but lost the personal curation and active maintenance. LLM Wiki reclaims those properties at the individual level.
